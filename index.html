This wasn't that. I jiggled that steering wheel like it was a vending machine holding a stuck bag of Funyuns. Nothing. There I was, sitting in my dad's minivan, trying to figure out what to do next. I could find a payphone and call my dad at work, but then what? I had his car. What was he going do? I could find a payphone and call the police, but what were they going do? Come down and arrest me for being such a loser? So, I just sort of hung my head in defeat like Charlie Brown after a baseball game, and that's when I saw a baby pacifier on the passenger seat. At that time, there were no babies in the family. I thought about that, about the baby pacifier, for about a tenth of a second, and while I was thinking about it, the side door of the minivan slid open, and a woman started loading up a baby into a heretofore unnoticed infant car seat in the back seat. I could see this clearly in the rearview mirror. You got it a little faster than I did: This was not my dad's minivan. As you might imagine, this was kind of an awkward moment for me. I couldn't say anything without alerting this woman to my presence. But I also couldn't exit the vehicle without alerting this woman to my presence. Then I sort-of hung out on the periphery of both vehicles until the woman with the baby had pulled away, lest she wonder why I was also walking away from my "wife."
